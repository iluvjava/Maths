Extra Stuff

Notice that, the sin⁡\sinsin function are always odd, and we are approximating it on the interval [0,L][0,L][0,L], and if we look at the series on the [−L,L][-L, L][−L,L], we get an odd function, and then it will periodically repeat, just like how sin⁡\sinsin is supposed to behave.

The series is only equal to F(x)F(x)F(x) on the [0,L][0, L][0,L] interval.

Over shoot

One the boundary, x=0,x=Lx = 0, x = Lx=0,x=L, there is the Gibbs Phenomena[4], it's over shooting on the edge just by a tiny bit whenever the function is not continuous when we augment it periodically, as n→∞n\rightarrow \inftyn→∞, the width of the over shoot gets infinitely small. Note: It's a fixed amount of overshoot, it's not shooting to infinity or anything like that.

[[Dirichlet Theorem]]

At the point of discontinuity, the Fourier Series is going to take the average between the points of discontinuity. Yes, it is not continuous regardless of the series being the sum of continuous functions. This occurs together with the Gibbs Phenomena.
