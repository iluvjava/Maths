Intro

The ODE we are solving is:

∂x[(1−x2)∂x[y]]+ηy=0(1)\partial_x[(1 - x^2)\partial_x[y]] + \eta y = 0 \tag{1} ∂x​[(1−x2)∂x​[y]]+ηy=0(1)

This is also called the associated Legendre equation in spherical coordinates, but with the case where m=0m = 0m=0. This means that there is no affect by the longitude direction, meaning that Φ(ϕ)\Phi(\phi)Φ(ϕ) is a constant.

To solve, we are going to use the Power Series expansion centered at a point. Let's call that point bbb.

However, we will need to take care of convergence of the series with more care, since the power centered at x=0x = 0x=0 might not converge at x=±1x = \pm 1x=±1.

We can still do that on the singularity, but that means we are going to use the [[Frobenius Method]], which could be a pain to deal with because of the extra multiplier at the front.
