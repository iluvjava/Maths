Last time we had the problem of the Gravitational Density and potential PDE as an example where Green's Function is used.

Here we will be modeling another PDEs, that is non-homogeneous over an unbounded domain.

Let's find a location where the δ\deltaδ is zero. And when that happens, we have a Homogeneous PDE, but at the point where it's not, we have a non-homogeneous PDE.

Can GGG be a delta function in the time dimension?

∂t[G]\partial_t[G]∂t​[G] will not be a delta function, and it will have more singularities [2], but on the RHS there is the delta function. This can't be possible. Therefore GGG is not a delta function on the time dimension.
