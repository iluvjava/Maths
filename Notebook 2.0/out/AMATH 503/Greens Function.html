[[Eigenfunction Expansion]]. In addition of using the Eigenfunction Expansion method, we have another alternative for solving the None-Homogeneous PDEs.

But this time, it's used for Solving None-Homogeneous equation in the infinite domain.

This method is described in chapter 8 of the lecture, and it's a pretty recent method to be honest.

Let's take a look.

Intro

It's used in Aeo-dynamics and Quantum Field Theory.

It's used for solving Non-Homogeneous PDEs under the Infinite Domain.

Warning:

We will be dealing with the Dirac Delta Function

Let L\mathbb{L}L be Sturm Loiuville Operator, which means that:

Consider the folling Non-homogeneous PDE:

Let's consider another probem:

And let's assume that the solution for GGG is better than the original problem, then the solution to the original problem can be recovered by:[1]

u(x)=∫abf(ξ)G(x,ξ)dξ  ⟹  L(u)[x]=L[∫abf(ξ)G(x,ξ)dξ](x)=∫abf(ξ)L[G(x,ξ)](x)dξ=∫abf(ξ)δ(x−ξ)dξ=f(x)\begin{aligned} u(x) &= \int_{a}^{b} f(\xi)G(x, \xi) d\xi \\ \implies \mathbb{L}(u)[x] &= \mathbb{L} \left[ \int_{a}^{b} f(\xi)G(x, \xi) d\xi \right](x) \\ &= \int_{a}^{b} f(\xi)\mathbb{L}[G(x, \xi)](x) d\xi \\ &= \int_{a}^{b} f(\xi)\delta(x - \xi) d\xi \\ &= f(x) \end{aligned} u(x)⟹L(u)[x]​=∫ab​f(ξ)G(x,ξ)dξ=L[∫ab​f(ξ)G(x,ξ)dξ](x)=∫ab​f(ξ)L[G(x,ξ)](x)dξ=∫ab​f(ξ)δ(x−ξ)dξ=f(x)​

Interpretation?

We assume that the solution for the whole non-homoegenous is a super position of many infinitely many tiny solution, as in the case of the equation for GGG.

And solving the system wrt to GGG and delta function, and then make a super position of all of them using the RHS of the Non-homogeneous solution will provide us with the full solution to the original problem.
