At the Boundary

In the discussion of the Cosine Fourier series, we know that the representation of a sin⁡\sinsin series is going to take the average at the boundary. This is the [[Dirichlet Theorem]].

However, when representing a constant of y=100y = 100y=100, the cosine series will be OK, both inside and outside of the interval.

So the Reasoning is gonna be like this:

  • By the symmetric property of the basis function, the cosine function is exactly the same as y=100y = 100y=100, and it's the same on the interval [−L,L][-L, L][−L,L].
  • Hence, the series takes value 100 on the interval [−L,L][-L, L][−L,L].
  • And then, by the periodicity of the function, we have that the series the same everywhere.
  • Therefore, the cosine series is just gonna give us: b0=100b_0 = 100b0​=100, and then all the other terms are zeros.
