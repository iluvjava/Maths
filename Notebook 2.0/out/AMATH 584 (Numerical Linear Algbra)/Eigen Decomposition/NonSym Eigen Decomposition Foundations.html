Lemma: Block Triangular Matrices

Consider a matrix AAA that is squared real matrix consisting of block of square matrices in the following form:

A=[B1,1B1,2⋯B2,2⋯⋱Bm,m]\begin{aligned} A = \begin{bmatrix} B_{1, 1} & B_{1, 2} & \cdots & \\ & B_{2, 2}& \cdots & \\ & & \ddots & \\ & & & B_{m, m} \end{bmatrix} \end{aligned} A=⎣⎡​B1,1​​B1,2​B2,2​​⋯⋯⋱​Bm,m​​⎦⎤​​

Then:

det(A)=∏i=1mdet(Bi,i)\text{det}(A) = \prod_{i = 1}^m \text{det}(B_{i, i}) det(A)=i=1∏m​det(Bi,i​)

Therefore, the eigenvalues of the matrix AAA is the union of all the eigenvalues of block diagonals of the matrix AAA.

Schur Decomposition

Here we are going to sate the results, for the proof for it, consult: [[Schur Decomposition]] for more information.

For any Matrix AAA, There eixsts a Unitary Matrix VVV such that:

VTAV=TV^TAV = T VTAV=T

Where TTT is upper triangular.

Reader please observe:

We assume that the matrix AAA is real,and in that sense, the matrix might have complex eigenvalues, which implies that the size of TTT is larger than AAA. When AAA is real and we are only decomposing with real eigenvalues, then it's called a "Quasi-Upper Triangular form".

Real Schur form and Quasi-Upper Triangular Form

We are considering real matrix that has pairs of conjugate Eigenvaues.

VTAV=TV^TAV = T VTAV=T

Where VVV is unitary.

Def: Quasi-Upper Triangular Form

A matrix VVV is said to be Quasi-Upper Triangular if the matrix has blocks of 2 by 2 matrices on the diagonals, and they contain pairs of conjugate eigenvalues for the matrix.
