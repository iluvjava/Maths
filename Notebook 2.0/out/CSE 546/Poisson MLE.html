We are going to derive the MLE for the poisson distribution [[Maximal Likelihood Estimator]]

We also discussed the generalized linear model for poisson distribution in [[Generalized Linear Model]], as an example, and putting it into an optimization problem in the optimization class.

Intro

The model is given by:

Poi(x∣λ)=exp⁡(−λ)λxx!\text{Poi}(x|\lambda) = \exp(-\lambda) \frac{\lambda^x}{x!} Poi(x∣λ)=exp(−λ)x!λx​

We are going to shoe that the best λ\lambdaλ that maximizes the likelihood is just the average of all the observed samples.

The poisson distribution models the probability of a number of events occuring during a fixed number of times.

Like, how many people are likely to visit the shop a day, or an particular hour of the day, or a particular moment of the day, etc.

Getting the Maximal Likelihood Estimator

Assumes that NNN observations of the number of occurrence during a particular time interval has been observed, and it's like: x1,x2,⋯xNx_1, x_2, \cdots x_Nx1​,x2​,⋯xN​.

The Likelihood:

∏n=1NPoi(xn∣λ)\prod_{n = 1}^{N} \text{Poi}(x_n|\lambda) n=1∏N​Poi(xn​∣λ)

Taking the log on the expression we have:

∑n=1Nlog⁡(Poi(xn∣λ))\sum_{n = 1}^{N} \log\left( \text{Poi}(x_n|\lambda) \right) n=1∑N​log(Poi(xn​∣λ))

So then it's like:

∑n=1N(−λ+xnln⁡(λ)+log⁡(xn!))\sum_{n = 1}^{N} \left( -\lambda + x_n\ln(\lambda) + \log(x_n!) \right) n=1∑N​(−λ+xn​ln(λ)+log(xn​!))

And notice that, only some of the terms are relevant to the value of λ\lambdaλ, and hence the best estimator will be given as:

λ+=argmaxλ{−Nλ+ln⁡(λ)∑i=1N(xn)}\lambda^+ = \underset{\lambda}{\text{argmax}} \left\lbrace -N\lambda + \ln(\lambda) \sum_{i = 1}^{N} \left( x_n \right) \right\rbrace λ+=λargmax​{−Nλ+ln(λ)i=1∑N​(xn​)}

Apply the derivative wrt to λ\lambdaλ for the inside, setting it to zero, we have:

∂λ[−Nλ+ln⁡(λ)∑i=1N(xn)]=0\partial_\lambda \left[ -N\lambda + \ln(\lambda) \sum_{i = 1}^{N} \left( x_n \right) \right] = 0 ∂λ​[−Nλ+ln(λ)i=1∑N​(xn​)]=0

−N+∑n=1Nxnλ+=0-N + \frac{\sum_{n = 1}^{N}x_n}{ \lambda^+ } = 0 −N+λ+∑n=1N​xn​​=0

  ⟹  λ+=∑n=1NxnN\implies \lambda^+ = \frac{\sum_{n = 1}^{N}x_n}{N} ⟹λ+=N∑n=1N​xn​​
