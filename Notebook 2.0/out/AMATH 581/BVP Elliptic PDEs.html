Boundary Conditions

For the following discussion, we will be only focusing on boundary conditions in the region of a rectangle on the grid.

Ω=[a,b]×[b,c]\Omega = [a, b]\times [b,c] Ω=[a,b]×[b,c]

Let's also assume that the functions is in 2d and it's time depend, expressed as ψ(x,y,t)\psi(x, y, t)ψ(x,y,t) to be precise.

Dirichlet Boundary:

ψ(a,y)=dψ(b,y)=g∀  y∈[b,c]\psi(a, y) = d \quad \psi(b, y) = g \quad \forall \; y\in [b, c] ψ(a,y)=dψ(b,y)=g∀y∈[b,c]

Where ddd and ggg are constants, and the same could be applied to the line where y=b,cy = b, cy=b,c

Neuman Boundary:

∂yψ(a,y)=d∂yψ(b,y)=g∀y∈[b,c]\partial_y \psi(a, y) = d \quad \partial_y\psi(b, y) = g \quad \forall y\in [b, c] ∂y​ψ(a,y)=d∂y​ψ(b,y)=g∀y∈[b,c]

So it involves some kind of derivatives.

Robin Boundary:

Any Linear combination of the Dirichlet Boundary conditions and the Robin Boundary conditions.

Periodic Boundary

ψ(a,y)=ψ(b,y)∀  y∈[c,d]\psi(a, y) = \psi(b, y) \quad \forall \; y\in [c,d] ψ(a,y)=ψ(b,y)∀y∈[c,d]

And the same thing for the other 2 boundary and it's like:

ψ(x,c)=ψ(x,d)∀  x∈[a,b]\psi(x, c) = \psi(x, d) \quad \forall \; x\in [a,b] ψ(x,c)=ψ(x,d)∀x∈[a,b]

And it can be repeated with derivative as the boundary conditions too.
