This is especially salient when high order off derivatives exists in the partial differential equation.

Stiff PDEs System

This happens when the CFL has extreme properties where we need to decrease the spatial stepping Δt\Delta tΔt much faster than the Spatial Discretization, and that is when it's called a stiff system.

And example of the stiff system is:

ut=−Cuxxxxu_t = -Cu_{xxxx} ut​=−Cuxxxx​

And for the 4th order equation, λ\lambdaλ will make the relation that:

Δt<Δx4C\Delta t < \frac{\Delta x^4}{C} Δt<CΔx4​

And this is not good because Δt\Delta tΔt will get too small to compute.

This is still a big problem in the case of Spectral Method. This is the case because kx4kx^4kx4 is a huge number, depending on the spatial discretization of NNN. An ODEs system with a huge huge numbers inside is still a stiff system, and the stability and accuracy will remain similar.
