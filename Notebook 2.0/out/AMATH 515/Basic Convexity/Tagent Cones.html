First-Order Approximation of Sets

The idea of the tangent cone is relevant to approximating the set with another set, we call "Cone", which arise for the understanding of strong duality in Linear programming too.

Cone

Cone a is a special type of subspace. Let CCC be an nonempty subset of a real linear space XXX, then:

Cone:

x∈C,λ≥0  ⟹  λx∈Cx \in C, \lambda \ge 0 \implies \lambda x \in C x∈C,λ≥0⟹λx∈C

Pointed Cone:

x∈C,−x∈C  ⟹  x=0xx \in C, - x \in C \implies x = 0_x x∈C,−x∈C⟹x=0x​

You can't place a double arrow inside the pointed cone.

Observe: If you have the std basis vector, then the positive weighted span of these vector is just the whole subspace, and hence, subspace is a special type of cone.

Tangent Cone

Let SSS be a nonempty subset of a real normed space (XXX, ∣∣∙∣∣||\bullet||∣∣∙∣∣). A vector in hhh is called a tangent vector to SSS of xˉ\bar{x}xˉ if there are sequence (xn)n∈N(x_n)_{n \in \mathbb{N}}(xn​)n∈N​ in SSS, such that:

xˉ=lim⁡n→∞xn\bar{x} = \lim_{n\rightarrow\infty} x_n xˉ=n→∞lim​xn​

And there exists another sequence of numbers λn\lambda_nλn​ such that:

h=lim⁡n→∞λn(xn−xˉ)h = \lim_{n\rightarrow \infty}\lambda_n(x_n - \bar{x}) h=n→∞lim​λn​(xn​−xˉ)

Note: This is a concept that is applicable to all sets, and in that sense, given any sets in vector space and a point that is in, or on the set (Geometrically), there will be a tangent cone for it.

Observe One of the choice for the λ\lambdaλ sequence is simply:

λn=∥xn−xˉ∥\lambda_n = \Vert x_n - \bar{x}\Vert λn​=∥xn​−xˉ∥

And in this case, the hhh vector is going to be a unit vector.

When the point xˉ\bar{x}xˉ is on the boundary and it's smooth, then the cone is a half space.
