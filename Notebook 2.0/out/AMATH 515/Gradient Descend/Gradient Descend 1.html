Introduction:

The major idea here is to use the β\betaβ Smoothness property of the function to approximate an global upper bound of the function given some points of approximation on the function.

Firstly let's define the notation:

Convergence with Beta Smootheness

Here, we are going to show how the iteration is going to converge under the β\betaβ smoothness assumption for the function.

Continuing with the statement (1)(1)(1), we have:

\begin{align}\tag{2} \frac{1}{2\beta}\Vert \nabla f(x_k)\Vert^2 &\le f(x_k) - f(x_{k + 1}) \\ \frac{1}{2\beta}\Vert \nabla f(x_{k - 1})\Vert^2 &\le f(x_{k - 1}) - f(x_{k}) \\ &(...) \\ \frac{1}{2\beta}\Vert \nabla f(x_0)\Vert^2 &\le f(x_0) - f(x_{1}) \\ \end{align}

Summing up all the terms we have a lot of things canceled out on the RHS of these inequalities, giving us:

12β∑j=0k(∥∇f(xj)∥2)≤f(x0)−f(xk+1)\frac{1}{2\beta} \sum_{j = 0}^{k} \left( \Vert\nabla f(x_j) \Vert^2 \right) \le f(x_0) - f(x_{k + 1}) 2β1​j=0∑k​(∥∇f(xj​)∥2)≤f(x0​)−f(xk+1​)

The sum of all the squared 2 norm of the gradient step we divided by the β\betaβ smoothness scale, we will have the change in the objective value for the algorithm as an lower bound. (A pretty pessimistic estimate)

Let's make the assumption that the xk+1x_{k + 1}xk+1​ is giving us inf(f)inf(f)inf(f), noted as f∗f^*f∗, then we can have:

The first term is the smallest gradient step, and the second terms is the average gradient step, and the third term is the upper bouund of the objective decrease.

Taking the square root at both side we have:

min⁡j∥∇f(xj)∥≤1k2β(f(x0)−f∗)\min_{j} \Vert \nabla f(x_j)\Vert \le \frac{1}{\sqrt{k}} \sqrt{ 2\beta \left( f(x_0) - f^* \right) } jmin​∥∇f(xj​)∥≤k​1​2β(f(x0​)−f∗)​

And then we have the following theorem for the descent algorithm.

The gradient descent for a β\betaβ smoothness function has the convergence rate of $$\mathcal{O}\left( \frac{1}{\sqrt{k}} \right)$$ This is sub linear, and it's the most pessimistic upper bound.

And notice some of the metrics that are involved here.

  1. The objective value of the function. measured by the distance from the current iterate on the objective value to the inf of the function.
  2. The minimal of the gradient.
  3. The distance of the optimal and the current iterate under the domain of the function.

As we add more and more assumption on the function, we can use more and more precise metric for the optimization.
