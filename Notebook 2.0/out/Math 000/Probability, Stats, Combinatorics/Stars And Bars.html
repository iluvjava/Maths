Here we introduce the basics of combinatorics.

Intro

Consider statements:

Given nnn distinguishable objects, we want to put them into kkk bins. How many are ways are there to put it?

I have kkk integers and I want to add them up to nnn, how many ways are there to add to that?

They are exactly the same thing.

Stars and Bars Statement

Define nnn and kkk as the same quantities in the statement of the problem above.

Now, we treat the sequences as a sequence of stars: ∗*∗ and bars ∗*∗. There are k−1k - 1k−1 bars and nnn stars in totally.

And then, the number of ways to arrange the stars and the bars is equivalent to the number of ways of putting the stars in between the bars.

That is the same as counting the combinations of bars in between the stars, or the stars in between the bars. So we can use the binomial coefficients to find out the number of combinations.
