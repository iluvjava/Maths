Intro

KL divergence is "Kullback-Leibler Divergence", it has the other name: "Relative Entropy".

Given a probability distribution space χ\chiχ and 2 probability distribution denoted as P,QP, QP,Q.

The KL divergence from QQQ to PPP is:

DKL(P∣∣Q)=∑x∈χP(x)log⁡2(P(x)Q(x))D_{KL}(P||Q) = \sum_{x\in \chi}^{}P(x) \log_2 \left( \frac{P(x)}{Q(x)} \right) DKL​(P∣∣Q)=x∈χ∑​P(x)log2​(Q(x)P(x)​)

And in the continuous case we have the expression:

DKL(P∣∣Q)=∫−∞∞p(x)log⁡2(p(x)q(x))dxD_{KL}(P||Q) = \int_{-\infty}^{\infty} p(x)\log_2 \left( \frac{p(x)}{q(x)} \right) dx DKL​(P∣∣Q)=∫−∞∞​p(x)log2​(q(x)p(x)​)dx

Note, usually: DKL(P∣∣Q)≠DKL(Q∣∣P)D_{KL}(P||Q)\ne D_{KL}(Q||P)DKL​(P∣∣Q)​=DKL​(Q∣∣P). This is definitely not a distance measure.

Key:

If we were to use some other kind of paramaterized distribution to represents a given observed distribution, how much information is lost? And this question is answered by the measure made by KL-Divergence.

Consider this:

DKL(P∣∣Q)=∑x∈χP(x)log⁡2(P(x)Q(x))=∑x∈χP(x)(log⁡2(P(x))−log⁡2(Q(x))=∑x∈χP(x)(log⁡2(1Q(x))−log⁡2(1P(x)))=E[log⁡2(1Q(x))−log⁡2(1P(x))]\begin{aligned} D_{KL}(P||Q) &= \sum_{x \in \chi}^{}P(x)\log_2 \left( \frac{P(x)}{Q(x)} \right) \\ &= \sum_{x\in \chi}^{} P(x)(\log_2(P(x)) - \log_2(Q(x)) \\ &= \sum_{x \in \chi}^{} P(x) \left( \log_2\left( \frac{1}{Q(x)} \right) - \log_2 \left( \frac{1}{P(x)} \right) \right) \\ &= \mathbb{E}\left[ \log_2\left( \frac{1}{Q(x)} \right) - \log_2 \left( \frac{1}{P(x)} \right) \right] \end{aligned} DKL​(P∣∣Q)​=x∈χ∑​P(x)log2​(Q(x)P(x)​)=x∈χ∑​P(x)(log2​(P(x))−log2​(Q(x))=x∈χ∑​P(x)(log2​(Q(x)1​)−log2​(P(x)1​))=E[log2​(Q(x)1​)−log2​(P(x)1​)]​
