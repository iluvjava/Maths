Intro

Schur's Decomposition: Every square matrix is similar to a Upper triangular matrix, and it's possible to do it with an unitary transformation.

A=QTTQA = Q^TTQ A=QTTQ

In the case when the original matrix AAA is complex, we use the complex conjugation of the matrix instead of the transpose of the matrix.

Claim 1:

All square matrices are similar to a upper triangular matrix and an unitary transform allows for the similarity.

The Spectral Theorem

Theorem

Hermitian Matrix (Complex Conjugate Transpose) are diagonalizable.

A=UTTUAT=(UTTU)TAT=UTTTUAT=A  ⟹  TT=T\begin{aligned} A & = U^TTU \\ A^T &= (U^TTU)^T \\ A^T &= U^TT^TU \\ A^T &= A \\ \implies T^T &= T \end{aligned} AATATAT⟹TT​=UTTU=(UTTU)T=UTTTU=A=T​

Hence TTT is diagonal.
