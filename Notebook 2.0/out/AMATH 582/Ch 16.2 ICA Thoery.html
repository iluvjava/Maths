Recap

We have s1s_1s1​, s2s_2s2​ to be the original signal that we are trying to separate. And x1,x2x_1, x_2x1​,x2​ are the channels of signals that we are getting receiving from the sensor.

x=Asx = As x=As

And the the we are trying to reverse the affect of AAA, the mixing matrix.

Let's say that:

A=UΣVTA = U\Sigma V^T A=UΣVT

By understanding the SVD, we are going to figure out how to reverse it.

  1. VTV^TVT This is a rotation matrix, it's going to take the signal, projecting it to the basis.
  2. Σ\SigmaΣ is the matrix that stretches things
  3. UUU is the matrix that rotates in the domain of the principal components.

Somehow, we need to undo the whole process with some help from variances.

Intuitively:

  1. We pick up the vector (A subspace) with the most amount of variances, and that will be the effect of the stretching down by the principal component. (Unwrapping UUU and principal σ\sigmaσ)
  2. That vector we pick will help use rotated back the whole data set.
  3. We minimize the kurtosis of the distributions, reversing the effect of VTV^TVT matrix (Approximately).

Notice that, we made the assumption that AAA is likely to be some kind of random matrix. Because that is the way that, the matrix AAA can have these kind of principal structure to it, σ\sigmaσ is not all equal, and the transformation is not special.

What is Krutosis:

This is a statistical measure on the peakness of a given probability distribution.

Kurtosis:

kurt(ϕ)=∑j=1N(([(x~1)i(x~2)j]T[cos⁡ϕsin⁡ϕ])4(x~1)j2+(x~2)j2)\text{kurt}(\phi) = \sum_{j = 1}^{N}\left( \frac{ \left( \begin{bmatrix} (\widetilde{x}_1)_i \\ (\widetilde{x}_2)_j \end{bmatrix}^T \begin{bmatrix} \cos{\phi} \\ \sin\phi \end{bmatrix} \right)^4 }{ (\widetilde{x}_1)_j^2 + (\widetilde{x}_2)_j^2 } \right) kurt(ϕ)=j=1∑N​⎝⎜⎜⎜⎜⎜⎜⎛​(x1​)j2​+(x2​)j2​([(x1​)i​(x2​)j​​]T[cosϕsinϕ​])4​⎠⎟⎟⎟⎟⎟⎟⎞​

And, we make the assumption that, under the alternative basis that describes the signal, the kurtosis is maximized (VERY VERY PEAK).

What is the intuition here? How is the signal represented in the 2D space and what kind of geometry does it went through?

  1. For each (x~1,i,x~2,i)(\widetilde{x}_{1, i}, \tilde{x}_{2, i})(x1,i​,x~2,i​) in space, we are projecting into another space, and measuring the kurtosis of the projected space.
  2. The signal has the shape of a square, center at the origin
  3. But the square has underwent a unitary transformation.
  4. We need to find the axis to project onto such that, kurtosis is maximized. And that is the diagonal of the square.
