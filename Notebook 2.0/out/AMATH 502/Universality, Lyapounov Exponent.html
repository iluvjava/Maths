Lyapunov Exponent

One trait of the chaotic system is sensitive dependence on initial condition (SDIC).

We want to quantify the SDIC. How sensitive?

Consider: 2 Initial Condition: x0,x0+δ0x_0, x_0 + \delta_0x0​,x0​+δ0​, where δ0\delta_0δ0​ is small.

After one iteration, the distance between the orbits is:

∣f(x0+δ0)−f(x0)∣|f(x_0 + \delta_0) - f(x_0)| ∣f(x0​+δ0​)−f(x0​)∣

2 Iterations:

∣f2(x0+δ0)−f2(x0)∣|f^2(x_0 + \delta_0) - f^2(x_0)| ∣f2(x0​+δ0​)−f2(x0​)∣

After N iteration, we have:

∣fN(x0+δ0)−fN(x0)∣≈∣δ0∣exp⁡(λN)|f^N(x_0 + \delta_0) - f^N(x_0)| \approx |\delta_0|\exp(\lambda N) ∣fN(x0​+δ0​)−fN(x0​)∣≈∣δ0​∣exp(λN)

Where, the ≈\approx≈ is an assumption that we made, it's not a bad assumption, by thinking about the linearization near the fixed point.

And it's not hard to see that the λ\lambdaλ is like:

λ≈1Nln⁡∣(fN)′(x0)∣\lambda \approx \frac{1}{N} \ln |(f^N)'(x_0)| λ≈N1​ln∣(fN)′(x0​)∣

Now, we would need to take the derivative of the deep composite function, and, we can use the orbit information to simplify the nested function of derivative[1], giving us:

Then, we consider the case that N→∞N\rightarrow \inftyN→∞, then we have the limit:

λ=lim⁡N→∞1N∑i=0N−1(ln⁡∣f′(xi)∣)\lambda = \lim_{N\rightarrow \infty} \frac{1}{N} \sum_{i = 0}^{N - 1}\left( \ln |f'(x_i)| \right) λ=N→∞lim​N1​i=0∑N−1​(ln∣f′(xi​)∣)

This is called the: "Lyapunov Exponent".

Most of the time, we will need to estimate it with computers, when the differential are easy, we can find the Lyapunov Exponent analytically.

And notice that, λ\lambdaλ depends on x0x_0x0​, but λ\lambdaλ is the same on a periodic orbit, also it's the same in the basin of attraction of any F.P, or Periodic Orbit.

When we are stable, the derivative at the orbit is going to be smaller than 1, which will give us ln⁡(≤1)\ln(\le 1)ln(≤1), which will give us the λ<0\lambda < 0λ<0, and if we have λ>0\lambda > 0λ>0, then this is an indicator for chaos.

This is called: SDIC, Sensitive Dependent on Initial conditions.
