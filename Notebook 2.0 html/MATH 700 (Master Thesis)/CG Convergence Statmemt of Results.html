Here we state the results of the rate of convergence for the Conjugate Gradient algorithm for:

  • Floating Numerics

Results are referenced with professor Greenbaum's work on this topic.

Exact Arithmatic

The relative error measured via the energy norm of the PSD matrix would be:

∥e(k)∥A∥e(0)∥A≤2(κ−1κ+1)k\frac{\Vert e^{(k)}\Vert_A}{ \Vert e^{(0)} \Vert_A} \le 2 \left( \frac{\sqrt{\kappa} - 1}{\sqrt{\kappa} + 1} \right)^k ∥e(0)∥A​∥e(k)∥A​​≤2(κ​+1κ​−1​)k

Where κ\kappaκ is the condition number of the matrix AAA, λn/λ1\lambda_n/\lambda_1λn​/λ1​. Where we assume λn,λ1\lambda_n, \lambda_1λn​,λ1​ are the largest and the smallest eigenvalues of the matrix AAA.

With the additional assumption that the largest eigen values is on its own, away from all the other small eigenvalues who are near the origin and close together to each other, a better bound for the convergence can be attained.

Eigenvalues are like:

λ1≤λ2≤⋯<<λn\lambda_1 \le \lambda_2 \le \cdots << \lambda_n λ1​≤λ2​≤⋯<<λn​

And the rate of convergence is:

∥e(k)∥A∥e(0)∥A≤2(κn−1−1κn−1+1)k−1\frac{\Vert e^{(k)}\Vert_A}{\Vert e^{(0)}\Vert_A} \le 2 \left( \frac{\sqrt{\kappa_{n - 1}} - 1}{\sqrt{\kappa_{n - 1} + 1}} \right)^{k - 1} ∥e(0)∥A​∥e(k)∥A​​≤2(κn−1​+1​κn−1​​−1​)k−1

Where κn−1\kappa_{n - 1}κn−1​ is:

κn−1=λn−1λ1\kappa_{n - 1} = \frac{\lambda_{n - 1}}{\lambda_1} κn−1​=λ1​λn−1​​

Which gives better covergence rate than when all eigenvalues are equally spread out. This is true because the condition number is significally smaller when the largest eigenvalues that lies outside of all the others are removed. An extreme case would be all previous n−1n - 1n−1 eigenvalues are equal to the same except for λn\lambda_nλn​.

The bound is tight and there eixsts a choice of the initial guess and matrix such that the relative errer hit the equality case for some iterations value kkk.
