Dirichlet Boundary Condition

Let's also call it: Homogeneous Type I Boundary condition.

Simulates when both ends of the rod is at the same temperature and it's fixed.

u(0,t)=u(L,0)=0u(0, t) = u(L, 0) = 0 u(0,t)=u(L,0)=0

Basis Function:

Xn(x)=sin⁡(λnx)λn=nπLn∈NX_n(x) = \sin\left( \lambda_n x \right) \quad \lambda_n = \frac{n\pi}{L} \quad n\in \mathbb{N} Xn​(x)=sin(λn​x)λn​=Lnπ​n∈N

The coefficients the coefficients of Fourier Sine Series. [[Fourier Sine Series (PDEs Flavor)]] of the initial condition.

Neumann Boundary Conditions

Simulates when both ends of the rod is not conducting, meaning that now flow of heat is happening there.

∂x[u](0,t)=∂x[u](L,t)=0\partial_x[u](0, t) = \partial_x[u](L, t) = 0 ∂x​[u](0,t)=∂x​[u](L,t)=0

Xn(x)=cos⁡(λnx)λn=Z≥0X_n(x) = \cos\left( \lambda_n x \right) \quad \lambda_n = \mathbb{Z}_{\ge 0} Xn​(x)=cos(λn​x)λn​=Z≥0​

And the coefficients is given by the Coefficients of the Fourier Series Cosine of the initial conditions.

Periodic Boundary Condition

Simulates heat conduction in a ring, so that both ends of the rod connect to itself. Meaning that the temperate equals and the flow of the heat also has to be equaled.

The ring is model by an closed en symmetric interval: [−L,L][-L, L][−L,L] and the point represented by ±L\pm L±L is the same point.

Therefore the boundary conditions will be:

u(−L,t)=u(L,t)∧∂x[u](L,t)=∂[u](−L,t)u(-L, t) = u(L, t) \wedge \partial_x[u](L, t) = \partial_[u](-L, t) u(−L,t)=u(L,t)∧∂x​[u](L,t)=∂[​u](−L,t)

And the basis functions will be like:

Xn(x)=Ansin⁡(λnx)+Bncos⁡(λnx)λn=nπLn∈ZX_n(x) = A_n\sin(\lambda_n x) + B_n\cos(\lambda_n x) \quad \lambda_n = \frac{n\pi }{L}\quad n\in \mathbb{Z} Xn​(x)=An​sin(λn​x)+Bn​cos(λn​x)λn​=Lnπ​n∈Z

Where, the coefficients AnA_nAn​, BnB_nBn​ are the Fourier Coefficients. [[Fourier Series (PDE Flavor)]]

Non-Homogeneous Dirichlet (Different Constant on Boundary)

Simulates the ends are set to constant temperate but they are not the same temperate.

u(0,t)=T1∧u(0,t)=T2u(0, t) = T_1 \wedge u(0, t) = T_2 u(0,t)=T1​∧u(0,t)=T2​

And it's not hard to see that the steady state solution will be:

us(x,t)=T2−T1Lx+T1u_s(x, t) = \frac{T_2 - T_1}{L}x + T_1 us​(x,t)=LT2​−T1​​x+T1​

And this is the homogeneous solution of the system, it will set ∂t\partial_t∂t​ into zero.

And it's not hard to see that the solution is a decomposition of 2 functions:

u(x,t)=un(x,t)+us(x,t)u(x, t) = u_n(x, t) + u_s(x, t) u(x,t)=un​(x,t)+us​(x,t)

And notice that u(x,t)−un(x,t)u(x, t) - u_n(x, t)u(x,t)−un​(x,t) is Dirichlet boundary conditions, therefore, we can solve it with Dirichlet boundary conditions and add back the homogeneous solution to get the full solution of the Equation.
